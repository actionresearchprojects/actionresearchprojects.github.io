Thank you!

ARC is a volunteer-led collective of experienced designers, builders, building scientists, and engineers working to keep people cool, healthy, and safe as the climate heats up - especially in socio-economically challenged regions across Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and other climate-vulnerable parts of the world.

Our mission is simple but urgent: to help communities develop buildings that remain comfortable and affordable without overly relying on expensive, energy-hungry systems. We work directly with local partners to design, test, and share practical, passive solutions that respond to extreme heat, humidity, and energy insecurity. But we have a realistic, science-informed long-term view, so we also recognise that as climate change becomes more extreme, we need to future proof buildings to allow future retrofit with optimised, robust and affordable mechanical cooling and ventilation systems - as and when passive techniques are no longer adequate. This represents a two-step process, where the first step must anticipate the second.

ARC’s growing core team of experts freely contribute their time and knowledge to this work because we believe in open, human-centred solutions grounded in science and real-world experience. We’re not just producing ideas - we’re building and testing them, training others, and sharing what works through open-access resources that others can adopt and adapt.

Whether you’re part of a company looking to make a meaningful impact through social responsibility, or an individual who wants to back practical action on climate resilience, your support goes directly where it’s needed: to knowledge-building and sharing, equipment, training, and collaboration on the ground - ARC runs a lean staff team supported by volunteer experts, minimising overheads.

Together, we can help ensure that buildings in the most challenging environments remain places of health, dignity, and refuge - today and for the future.
